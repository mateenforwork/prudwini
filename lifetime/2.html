Home > health_and_wellness > general_health > BCBSIL Volunteers Build Wheelchairs for Disabled Veterans

Last summer, 150 Blue Cross and Blue Shield of Illinois (BCBSIL) employee volunteers built 30 wheelchairs and gave them to the Edward Hines, Jr., Veterans Administration Hospital west of Chicago.

The wheelchairs will have a big impact.

Dan Bracker heads the Hines VA Hospital escort team of 31 employees and volunteers. They move hundreds of veterans every day in wheelchairs across the 147-acre Hines campus. Bracker is a 20-year Army sergeant first class veteran who “wanted to do something really meaningful when I retired from the military. That’s why I work at Hines.

“We have 200 wheelchairs now. So these 30 more will make a big difference,” Bracker said. “We can lower or even avoid time our vets now spend waiting for chairs to free up so we can get them where they need to go.”

Veterans live in the communities that BCBSIL serves, and many of its own employees are veterans. So events like this are very meaningful to our employees. They provide an opportunity to show appreciation for the service and sacrifice veterans have given.

Wheelchair Gift Thrilling

Mitra Gobin is chief of voluntary services for Hines. Gobin oversees donations, cash gifts and the volunteers who donate their time doing things like maintaining the hospital grounds.

He was at Chicago BCBSIL headquarters on June 28 when the wheelchairs were built. He offered thanks there and later spoke at the hospital when the chairs were dropped off.

“We’re always thrilled when we get tangible donations,” Gobin said. “When I told colleagues at Hines I was going to BCBSIL to say a few words of thanks, everybody in my office applauded.”

Mike Dolan drove the truck to deliver the wheelchairs. He got caught up in the moment. “My dad, Robert, was in the Navy Reserves. As a veteran, he would have been proud of what’s happening today,” he said, as his eyes welled up with tears.

BCBSIL Marine Veteran Helps

James Keathley was among the 150 volunteers who assembled the wheelchairs, helped load them on the delivery truck and then hopped onto the truck to unload them at the Hines VA Hospital.

Keathley is BCBSIL’s senior supervisor of protection and security and a Marine Corps veteran.

“I’m very proud we have this opportunity to be involved in an event supporting veterans’ needs,” Keathley said. “Delivering these chairs as a veteran and as an employee of the company just makes me feel good because I know this is a good thing for veterans here at Hines VA Hospital. It’s something we’re giving back to the veterans.”

Our company’s Supporters of Military Veterans leadership group and human resources vice president Ron Balsewich arranged the event. Balsewich also runs the In-Abled group at Blue Cross and Blue Shield.

The Blue Cross and Blue Shield veterans group is committed to reaching out in practical ways. For example, the Fisher House at Hines gets help from BCBSIL. Fisher House is a free home away from home for patients’ families, caregivers and loved ones. Its motto is “Because a Family’s Love Is Good Medicine.”

Blue Cross and Blue Shield is also committed to all people working at our company with physical and other challenges. Such challenges must not get in the way of vital daily contributions they make. Blue Cross and Blue Shield cares not only for those who protect America, but also for those working here.

Volunteers share what being part of this effort means to them.
